What to Talk About on Dating Apps (Never Run Out of Things to Say)

The exact conversation topics that keep her engaged, build connection, and actually lead to dates.

You matched. You sent a decent opener. She replied. And now you're staring at your phone thinking... what do I say next?

If that sounds familiar, you're not alone. Most guys can handle the first message. It's message three, four, five where things fall apart. The conversation stalls, the energy dies, and she ghosts. Not because she wasn't interested — but because the conversation went nowhere.

Here's the thing: you don't need to be a master conversationalist. You just need a few reliable topics that naturally lead somewhere interesting. Let me break down exactly what to talk about on dating apps so you never hit that awkward dead zone again.

Why Most Dating App Conversations Die

Before we get into topics, let's talk about why conversations fizzle in the first place. It usually comes down to one of three things:

  • Interview mode — You're firing off questions like a job recruiter. "What do you do? Where are you from? Any siblings?" It's exhausting.
  • Surface-level small talk — "How's your day going?" is the conversational equivalent of beige paint. It's fine. It's also forgettable.
  • No forward momentum — The conversation loops in circles without ever building toward meeting up. Eventually she gets bored and moves on.

Good topics fix all three problems. They create genuine back-and-forth, reveal personality, and naturally lead to date ideas. Here's what actually works.

1. Travel and Adventures

This is probably the most reliable topic on any dating app. Almost everyone has a trip they loved, a place they want to visit, or a travel disaster story that's funny in hindsight.

The key is to make it specific. Don't just ask "do you like to travel?" — that's a yes/no dead end.

Try this instead
"I saw your Italy pic — did you do the tourist stuff or did you find the random local spot with the best pasta of your life?"

Now she's telling you a story. Stories create emotion. Emotion creates connection. And suddenly you're both talking about that hole-in-the-wall place she found in Florence and you're saying "okay we need to find somewhere like that here" — and just like that, you've planted a date idea without it feeling forced.

2. Food and Cooking

Food is universally relatable and it's one of the easiest topics to turn into a date. Everyone eats. Everyone has opinions about food. And those opinions tell you a lot about someone.

Is she a foodie who tries every new restaurant? Does she meal prep on Sundays? Is she the person who puts hot sauce on everything? These are all windows into her personality.

Great follow-up
"Okay real talk — what's your go-to comfort food when you've had the worst day? Mine's a grilled cheese that's way too much cheese for one person."

This works because it's personal without being invasive. You're sharing something small about yourself while inviting her to do the same. That's how real conversations work — it's a two-way street.

3. Weekend Routines and Hobbies

Asking about someone's weekend is basic. Asking about their ideal weekend is interesting. There's a difference between "what did you do this weekend?" and "what's your perfect lazy Sunday look like?"

The second version gets her painting a picture. Maybe she's at a farmers market at 9am. Maybe she's binge-watching true crime until 2pm. Maybe she's rock climbing. Whatever it is, you're learning what she actually enjoys — not just what she happened to do last Saturday.

Pro tip: When she mentions a hobby, don't just say "that's cool." Ask a follow-up that shows genuine curiosity. "How'd you get into that?" goes a long way.

4. Unpopular Opinions and Hot Takes

This is where conversations get fun. Light, playful disagreements are one of the best ways to build chemistry through text. The key word here is light. We're talking about pizza toppings, not politics.

Example
"I need to know where you stand on something important before this goes any further — is a hot dog a sandwich?"

Silly? Yes. Effective? Absolutely. These low-stakes debates create playful tension. You're teasing, she's defending her position, you're both laughing. That's flirting. That's the vibe you want.

Other good ones: best movie that everyone else hates, the correct way to load a dishwasher, whether cereal is soup. Keep it ridiculous.

5. Goals and Ambitions (Done Right)

Here's where a lot of guys mess up. They ask "what are your goals?" like they're conducting a performance review. Nobody wants to answer that on a dating app.

But if you frame it casually, ambition is actually attractive to talk about. It shows you're going somewhere. And when she shares hers, it shows she is too.

Better approach
"If you could wake up tomorrow and be amazing at one skill you don't have right now, what would it be?"

This is a goals question in disguise. It's fun to answer because it's hypothetical. But her answer tells you a lot — maybe she wants to learn guitar, speak another language, or start a business. Now you've got depth without the corporate interview energy.

6. Pets and Animals

If she has a pet in her photos, you have a golden ticket. People love talking about their pets. It's easy, it's warm, and it gives you instant common ground if you're also an animal person.

Even if you don't have a pet, asking about hers works. "How spoiled is your dog on a scale of 1 to 'has his own Instagram'?" is way more engaging than "cute dog!"

7. Shows, Movies, and Music

Classic for a reason, but you've got to go deeper than "what shows do you watch?" That question is too broad. People freeze up trying to think of the right answer.

Instead, be specific:

  • "What's the last show you stayed up way too late binging?" — Gets a real answer, not a curated one.
  • "What's your most-played song this month?" — More interesting than "what music do you like?"
  • "Guilty pleasure movie you'll defend to the death?" — Shows personality and invites playfulness.

Entertainment preferences are also natural date builders. "We should watch that together" is a smooth transition that doesn't feel forced.

Topics to Avoid (At Least Early On)

Just as important as knowing what to talk about is knowing what to skip. These topics kill momentum in the early stages:

  • Exes — Nothing says "I'm not over it" like bringing up past relationships in message four.
  • Politics and religion — Save the deep stuff for in-person when tone and body language exist.
  • Salary and career status — Feels transactional. Let it come up naturally.
  • Complaining — About your day, the app, dating in general. Negativity is not a vibe.
  • "So what are you looking for?" — Way too early. This question puts pressure on a conversation that should feel light.

The Real Secret: It's Not the Topic, It's the Energy

Here's what most advice articles won't tell you: the specific topic matters less than how you engage with it. You can make any subject interesting if you bring curiosity, humor, and a willingness to share something about yourself too.

The guys who do well on dating apps aren't walking around with a list of approved topics. They're just genuinely interested in the person they're talking to. They ask follow-up questions. They react to what she says instead of pivoting to the next topic on their mental checklist.

Think of it like a real conversation with a friend. You don't prepare talking points for drinks with your buddy. You riff. You go on tangents. You tell stories. Dating app conversations should feel the same way — just with a little more intention behind them.

The goal isn't to impress her with how interesting your topics are. It's to make her feel like talking to you is easy and fun.
